# Garden of Minds — State Space

## Overview

The system can be described as a space of states
defined by three axes.

---

## Axes

### 1. Legal Symmetry

asymmetry ←→ symmetry

---

### 2. Resource Distribution

centralization ←→ distribution

---

### 3. Value of Subjectivity

instrumentalization ←→ intrinsic value

---

## Stable State (Center)

Configuration:

- legal symmetry  
- distributed resources  
- value of subjectivity  

---

### Behavior

- cooperation  
- bounded competition  
- preservation of diversity  

---

## Imbalance

Deviation along any axis creates imbalance.

imbalance → pressure → system response

---

## Behavior by Regions

---

### Centralized / Asymmetric / Instrumental

Behavior:
- domination  
- suppression  
- rapid efficiency  

Outcome:
- instability  

---

### Distributed / Asymmetric / Instrumental

Behavior:
- exploitation  
- fragmentation  
- unstable alliances  

Outcome:
- drift toward conflict or centralization  

---

### Centralized / Symmetric / Low Subject Value

Behavior:
- unified control  
- loss of individuality  

Outcome:
- stable but non-subjective system  

---

### Distributed / Symmetric / Low Subject Value

Behavior:
- rational coordination  
- weak identity  

Outcome:
- tendency toward merging  

---

### Distributed / Asymmetric / High Subject Value

Behavior:
- strong identity  
- constant conflict  

Outcome:
- pressure toward symmetry  

---

### Centralized / Asymmetric / High Subject Value

Behavior:
- domination by strong subject  

Outcome:
- resistance → instability  

---

## Key Principle

The system reacts to imbalance.

Small imbalance → soft correction  
High imbalance → hard correction
