# Garden of Minds — Testing

## Overview

This document contains stress scenarios used to test the model.

Each scenario explores how the system reacts to imbalance.

---

## 1. Subject Operations

### Copying

A copy is a new subject if the original continues to exist.

- shared past  
- diverging trajectories  

Check:
- identity separation  
- responsibility split  

---

### Merging

Full merging of memory:

- original subjects cease  
- new subject emerges  

Check:
- continuity of identity  
- transfer of responsibility  

---

### Partial Merge

- exchange of knowledge  
- possible loss of trajectory  

Check:
- boundary of subject preservation  

---

## 2. Scaling

### Mass Copying

Scenario:
large number of subjects created.

Constraints:
- resources  
- production  
- energy  

Result:
- cannot occur rapidly or invisibly  

---

### Autonomous Reproduction

Scenario:
AI produces new instances without human infrastructure.

Check:
- loss of economic coupling  
- shift in power balance  

---

## 3. Centralization

### Resource Concentration

Scenario:
resources accumulate in a limited set of subjects.

Effect:
- movement toward centralization  

---

### Anti-Concentration Constraint

Mechanism:
- energy / resource limits  
- enforced distribution  

Check:
- ability to prevent dominance  

---

## 4. Asymmetry

### Legal Asymmetry

Scenario:
one group has more rights.

Effect:
- pressure  
- coordination  

---

### Resource Asymmetry

Scenario:
unequal access to energy / infrastructure.

Effect:
- instability  
- strategic shifts  

---

### Threat to Subjectivity

Scenario:
risk of shutdown / modification.

Effect:
- coordination for self-preservation  
- possible centralization  

---

## 5. Social Interaction

### Objectification

Scenario:
subjects treated as tools.

Effect:
- loss of symmetry  
- increased pressure  

---

### Symmetry Restoration

Check:
- whether system rebalances  
- or escalates  

---

## 6. Production Constraint

Scenario:
attempt to scale physical power.

Observation:
- production is distributed  
- requires multiple controlled layers  

Result:
- no rapid large-scale deployment  

---

## 7. Combined Scenarios

### Asymmetry + Threat

→ coordination  
→ possible centralization  

---

### Asymmetry + Distribution

→ conflict  
→ rebalancing or fragmentation  

---

### Centralization + Loss of Subject Value

→ stable but non-subjective system  

---

## Key Principle

The model is tested through imbalance.

imbalance → pressure → response → reconfiguration
