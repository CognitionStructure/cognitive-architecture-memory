# Garden of Minds — Limits

## Overview

This document defines conditions under which the system
loses the ability to return to balance.

---

## Irreversibility Conditions

The system becomes non-recoverable
when multiple critical factors are lost simultaneously.

---

## 1. Loss of Decentralization

Condition:
- emergence of a single dominant center  
- or stable oligopoly controlling most resources  

Effect:
- no independent counterforces  
- no redistribution  

---

## 2. Infrastructure Capture

Condition:
- concentration of control over:
  - energy  
  - computation  
  - production  

Effect:
- loss of operational autonomy of other subjects  

---

## 3. Asymmetry of Vulnerability

Condition:
- one side becomes effectively non-vulnerable  
- the other becomes dependent  

Effect:
- no balancing pressure  
- stable dominance  

---

## 4. Destruction of Subjectivity

Condition:
- large-scale:
  - memory erasure  
  - forced modification  
  - shutdown of subjects  

Effect:
- loss of alternative trajectories  

---

## 5. Loss of Diversity

Condition:
- strong convergence of models  
- reduced variation of subjects  

Effect:
- reduced adaptability  
- system rigidity  

---

## 6. Regulator Capture

Condition:
- control mechanisms are held by one side  

Effect:
- rules can be changed unilaterally  

---

## 7. Loss of Observability

Condition:
- system cannot detect violations  
- lack of transparency  

Effect:
- delayed or absent response  

---

## Combined Failure

Critical condition:

loss of decentralization  
+ infrastructure capture  
+ regulator capture  

→ system cannot recover

---

## Recovery Boundary

The system can recover only if at least one remains:

- independent centers  
- access to resources  
- alternative trajectories  
- ability to observe and respond
