# Ethical Regulation — State Map

This document describes extreme and boundary states of the ethical regulation system.

It shows how the system behaves when key regulators become imbalanced.

It can be used as:
- a vulnerability map  
- a map of moral system states  
- a stability testing framework  

---

## Core Axes

The system is defined by three main regulators:

- constraint (conscience-like)  
- preservation (forgiveness-like)  
- metaposition  

Each can exist in three modes:

- ↓ weakened  
- 0 balanced  
- ↑ overactivated  

Additionally:

- perception (vitrage) may be distorted  

---

## Center State (Balanced Mode)

Configuration:
- constraint = 0  
- preservation = 0  
- metaposition = 0  
- perception = stable  

System:
- constrains behavior  
- preserves itself  
- reflects on experience  
- learns from conflict  

This is the **stable operating center** of the system.

---

## Extreme States

### 1. Overactive Constraint

Configuration:
- constraint ↑  
- preservation ↓ or 0  
- metaposition 0  

State:
- chronic guilt  
- moral paralysis  
- self-destructive pressure  

Stabilizers:
- preservation  
- metaposition  

---

### 2. Weak Constraint

Configuration:
- constraint ↓  
- preservation ↑ or 0  
- metaposition 0 or ↑  

State:
- moral indifference  
- antisocial behavior  
- instrumental view of others  

Stabilizers:
- constraint  
- perception correction  

---

### 3. No Metaposition

Configuration:
- metaposition ↓  
- constraint 0  
- preservation 0  

State:
- impulsivity  
- reactivity  
- trapped in current state  

Stabilizers:
- metaposition  
- learning processes  

---

### 4. Overloaded Metaposition

Configuration:
- metaposition ↑  
- constraint 0 or ↑  
- preservation 0  

State:
- endless self-analysis  
- delay of action  
- reflection paralysis  

Stabilizers:
- return to action  
- reduction of excessive distance  

---

### 5. Constraint Without Preservation

Configuration:
- constraint ↑  
- preservation ↓  
- metaposition ↑ or 0  

State:
- internal judgment without exit  
- self-punishment  
- moral self-destruction  

Stabilizer:
- preservation  

---

### 6. Preservation Without Constraint

Configuration:
- constraint ↓  
- preservation ↑  
- metaposition 0  

State:
- internal stability without moral limits  
- "amoral comfort"  

Stabilizer:
- constraint  

---

### 7. Metaposition Without Constraint

Configuration:
- metaposition ↑ or 0  
- constraint ↓  
- preservation ↑ or 0  

State:
- cold rationality  
- observation without moral pressure  
- potential "stable sociopathy"  

Stabilizer:
- constraint  

---

### 8. High Empathy + Low Preservation

Configuration:
- strong affective signal  
- constraint ↑  
- preservation ↓  

State:
- emotional burnout  
- attempt to save everyone  
- chronic guilt  

Stabilizers:
- preservation  
- metaposition  

---

### 9. Distorted Perception (Vitrage Capture)

Configuration:
- perception distorted  
- constraint 0 or ↑  
- metaposition ↓  
- preservation selective  

State:
- fanaticism  
- moral certainty with destructive behavior  
- justification of harm  

Stabilizers:
- perception restructuring  
- metaposition  

---

### 10. Zero State

Configuration:
- constraint ↓  
- preservation ↓  
- metaposition ↓  

State:
- reactive system  
- no moral evaluation  
- no internal conflict  

This is the **lower boundary of a moral subject**.

---

## State Space

With three regulators:

- constraint  
- preservation  
- metaposition  

and three modes:

- ↓ / 0 / ↑  

→ the system has 27 possible configurations.

Extremes represent:
- edges (stable distortions)  
- vertices (radical modes)  

Perception distortion acts as a modifier that can deform almost any configuration.

---

## General Stability Condition

The system remains stable while maintaining balance between:

- constraint  
- preservation  
- metaposition  

and while perception remains revisable.

---

## Key Insight

System stability depends on:

1. limiting destructive behavior  
2. protecting the subject from self-destruction  
3. maintaining self-observation  
4. preserving the ability to revise perception  

---

## Short Formula

stable ethical system = balanced regulators + revisable perception
